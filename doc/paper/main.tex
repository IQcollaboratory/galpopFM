\begin{abstract}
    We present the Empirical Dust Attenuation (\eda) model, an empirical
    framework for applying dust attenuation to simulated galaxy populations.
    The \eda~uses \tks{an observationally motivated parametrization of attenuation}
    %a parameterization of the attenuation curve based on observations 
    and a statistical prescription for assigning the curves that 
    includes correlations with galaxy properties. We apply the \eda~\tks{separately/individually} to three
    state-of-the-art hydrodynamical simulations --- SIMBA, \tks{IllustrisTNG}, 
    and EAGLE --- and forward model their optical and UV color-magnitude
    relations, $(\gr) - M_r$ and $(\fnuv)-M_r$. Then we compare them to \tks{volume limited SDSS and GALEX 
    observations} using simulation-based inference. 
    %For TNG and EAGLE, with the
    %\eda~we accurately reproduce the SDSS color magnitude relations. Meanwhile, for
    \tks{When the \eda~is included we accurately reproduce the color magnitude relations
    of the observational sample for EAGLE and TNG.}
    \tks{For} SIMBA, we struggle to reproduce observations due to an overprediction \tks{or overbrightness(?)}
    of starburst galaxies. The bestfit \eda~s for TNG and EAGLE also predict
    attenuation--slope relations and attenuation curves of star-forming
    galaxies in good agreement with observations. Beyond reproducing observations,
    the \eda~also sheds light on dust attenuation in quiescent galaxies, which
    is challenging to measure \tks{observationally.}
    %in observations. Quiescent 
    \tks{We find that quiescent} galaxies have
    significant UV and optical \tks{attenuation (?)} with shallow %attenuation 
    curves\tks{, in contrast to star-forming galaxies}. The \eda, more
    broadly, reveals the connections between galaxy properties and dust
    attenuation: more massive galaxies %have
    \tks{require} higher overall dust attenuation
    while galaxies with higher %SSFR 
    \tks{specific star formation rates} have steeper attenuation curves. In
    addition to improving our understanding of dust in galaxies, our results
    underscore the advantages of the \eda~and the forward modeling approach. 
\end{abstract}
